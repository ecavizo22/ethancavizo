About
The Background
My background blends business, marketing, and negotiation, shaped by hands-on NIL experience managing four student-athletes, a global marketing internship at a tennis club in Barcelona, and a lifelong connection to baseball. I work at the intersection of athlete representation and brand strategy — partnering directly with athletes and brands to build authentic collaborations that drive exposure and revenue.

Baseball has been part of my life since I was a kid. I grew up playing the game, and my high school — known for producing Division I talent — gave me an early understanding of what it takes to compete at a high level and the relationships that come with it. That foundation is what drives my long-term goal: MLB player representation. I'm drawn to baseball because it rewards patience and strategy — fully guaranteed contracts, no salary cap, and careers that span decades when managed right. As the sport shifts toward more marketable, brand-conscious players, I see a gap where my NIL and storytelling background can make a real difference in growing athletes both on and off the field.

My path is intentional. I'm building experience first — starting in NIL, pursuing agency internships, and developing the recruiting, marketing, and deal management skills that translate directly into certified agent work. Down the road, I plan to pursue the credentials needed to represent players at the highest level, whether that's a master's degree or a JD, depending on where the career leads. But right now, the priority is reps, relationships, and results.

My approach is simple: build the player before the paycheck. Most agents focus on the contract. I focus on the person — their story, their brand, their market value beyond the stat line. I believe the best representation starts with understanding who an athlete is off the field, then building a strategy that makes them impossible for brands and fans to ignore. When the marketability is there, the deals follow.
